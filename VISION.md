# Mini Archive

## Vision

Mini Archive exists to preserve the history, craftsmanship, and provenance of physical miniatures.

Every miniature tells a story.

That story should not disappear because a social media account was deleted, a website expired, a collector passed away, or ownership changed.

Mini Archive provides every miniature with a permanent digital identity that can accompany it throughout its entire lifetime.

Our goal is not to replace social media.

Our goal is to preserve what social media cannot.

---

# Core Principles

## 1. The Miniature Comes First

The miniature is the primary entity.

Artists, owners, collections, competitions, and campaigns all revolve around the miniature—not the other way around.

A miniature can outlive its painter, owner, or even the game system it belongs to.

Its history deserves to remain intact.

---

## 2. Every Miniature Deserves a Permanent Identity

Every archived miniature receives a unique Archive ID.

The Archive ID is permanent.

It never changes.

Ownership may change.

Names may change.

Paint schemes may change.

The Archive ID remains constant.

---

## 3. Preserve History

Mini Archive exists to document a miniature's journey.

Examples include:

- Creation
- Painting
- Progress photos
- Paint recipes
- Competition entries
- Awards
- Campaign history
- Ownership transfers
- Restoration
- Publications
- Retirement

History should be additive.

Nothing meaningful should be erased.

---

## 4. Technology Is Not The Product

QR codes.

NFC tags.

RFID.

Certificates.

These are methods of accessing an Archive Record.

They are not Mini Archive itself.

Mini Archive is the permanent record.

---

## 5. Platform Agnostic

Mini Archive is not tied to any manufacturer or game.

Examples include:

- Warhammer
- Dungeons & Dragons
- Marvel Crisis Protocol
- Battletech
- Historical Miniatures
- Scale Models
- Busts
- Dioramas
- 3D Printed Models

Every physical miniature deserves a history.

---

## 6. Authenticity Through Transparency

Mini Archive should make it easier—not harder—to verify a miniature's history.

Verification should be based on documented evidence rather than claims.

Trust is earned through transparency.

---

## 7. Ownership Is Temporary

Owners change.

Collectors change.

Collections change.

The Archive Record belongs to the miniature.

Ownership is simply another event in its lifetime.

---

## 8. Built For Decades

Mini Archive should be designed with longevity in mind.

Archive Records should remain accessible long after social media trends, technologies, and platforms change.

The goal is preservation measured in decades—not product cycles.

---

## 9. Community Before Commerce

Mini Archive exists to benefit the miniature hobby.

Monetization should support the platform—not define it.

Every business decision should strengthen the archive rather than compromise its purpose.

---

## 10. Every Miniature Has A Story

Whether it is a first painted Space Marine or a Slayer Sword winning masterpiece, every miniature deserves the opportunity to have its story preserved.

No miniature is too small.

No artist is too unknown.

Every miniature has a place in Mini Archive.
